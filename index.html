About Me

I'm from Saint Simons Island, Georgia and I'm passionate about computer science. With a Bachelor's and Master's in Computer Science from Georgia Southern University, I'm currently pursuing my PhD at Augusta University. My research focuses on software vulnerabilities, classroom attention tracking, and AI applications in education. I'm dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in computer science and its applications in educational settings.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I'm committed to promoting computer science as a career. Recently, I had the opportunity to speak with hundreds of high school students at the Big Tide Changemakers Summit 2025, sharing my experiences and insights about the field of computer science.

Recent Achievements

Recipient of DoD Cyber Service Academy Scholarship

Awarded the prestigious Department of Defense (DoD) Cyber Service Academy (CSA) Scholarship, which supports students pursuing careers in cybersecurity and information assurance. This scholarship recognizes academic excellence and commitment to addressing the nation's critical cybersecurity needs.

Third Place at Innovate 2024

Pitched my thesis work, NiCATS (Non-intrusive Classroom Attention Tracking System), at Innovate 2024. The presentation focused on using NiCATS to help diagnose children with learning disabilities, showcasing the potential real-world impact of my research.

Speaker at Big Tide Changemakers Summit 2024 and 2025

Engaged with hundreds of high school students at the Big Tide Changemakers Summit 2024 and 2025, promoting computer science as a career path and sharing insights about the field's potential and opportunities.
